12 Cognitive Biases. ^
The 12 Biases Explained:
1. Anchoring Bias (0:18): Over-relying on the first piece of information received when making decisions.
2. Availability Heuristic Bias (1:21): Overestimating the importance of information that is easily recalled or frequently presented in media.
3. Bandwagon Effect (2:21): Doing or believing something primarily because others do (following the crowd).
4. Choice Supportive Bias (3:08): A tendency to defend your own past choices by ignoring their faults and highlighting their benefits.
5. Confirmation Bias (3:50): Focusing only on information that confirms pre-existing beliefs and ignoring contradictory data.
6. Ostrich Bias (4:29): Subconsciously ignoring negative information or problems in the hope they will disappear.
7. Outcome Bias (5:18): Judging the quality of a decision solely by its result rather than the reasoning process used at the time.
8. Overconfidence (6:11): Becoming too confident due to past successes, leading to reliance on gut feelings rather than facts.
9. Placebo Bias (6:52): Believing something will have an effect can actually cause that effect to occur.

10. Survivorship Bias (7:44): Making judgments based only on successful "survivors" while ignoring the vast majority of failures.
11. Selective Perception (8:31): Filtering out information that contradicts your beliefs while focusing on what matches your frame of reference.
12. Blind Spot Bias (9:06): The inability to recognize your own biases while being quick to point out biases in others.
